Internet Penetration

As technology has advanced, the percentage of people using the Internet has increased dramatically. The entire world is now considered to have full Internet coverage. In United Arab Emirates, 100% of the citizens have access to the Internet in 2020. However, it is important to note that the internet penetration rate in Africa is still relatively low compared to other regions of the world.

The Growth of Internet Users(%) in term of Region and Income Group

North America has a significant lead in internet penetration, with over 80% of the population having access to the internet in 2016. Other regions, such as Western Sahara and South Asia have not yet reached this milestone, with internet penetration rates below 40%.
Similarly,High Income Countries have a significant advantage in internet penetration compared to other income groups, with over 90% of the population in high-income countries having access to the internet, compared to just 21% of the population in Low Income Countries.

Moving the slider, we can see that the percentage of internet users has grown rapidly over time. However, the radial chart shows that the countries with the highest number of internet users are not the same as the countries with the highest percentage of internet users.
United States had the highest number of internet users before 2008, but China surpassed it in 2008 and has remained the top country ever since. This is because China has a much larger population than United States.

Cellular subscriptions have grown significantly over the past decade, but at a slower rate than broadband subscriptions. This trend is likely to slow down even further in the coming years, due to the increasing penetration of broadband and the saturation of the cellular market.

An interesting fact that can be observed from the bubble chart is that there are more people who tend to have more than one cellular subscription.


Tracking Internet User Growth: Waterfall Chart Analysis

Finally, looking at the country-specific data provided, it's clear that China saw continuous Internet penetration growth, while the United States faced challenges from 2007 to 2015. This can be linked to the housing bubble collapse, which caused the Great Recession. This shift is visualized in the 2007 timeline when China surpassed the United States in Internet user numbers.
